  • Oh putty, thou sweet smelly thing, How green thy existence has been. Thy life in my armpit, was hardly to be seen, till knocked out by my unfeeling hands, Onto Cruel worlds below of Thicksoled Shoes and sands.
  • Of all puttys that from my armpits have come, Thou, my love, was most precious to me, But i've lost thee in papers and chewing gum, that the grounds below, littered with, be. It hath made me so glum.
  • Oh, green putty, thy beauty to see, Like the moon shining on a Thingamajee, Makes me blush with pleasure, a bright yellow color, Thy smell is so sweet, to taste you I'll forfeit.
  • Oh, putty, to see you again, What i will not give, the question begs answer, Be it, gold, or silver, or an ivory dancer, Anything at all, to get you back again. So that, in my armpit, you can safely remain
